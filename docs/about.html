About JENGE Impact Fund

Empowering Kenyan Youth Through Solidarity-Driven Enterprises

Our Mission

JENGE Impact Fund is dedicated to creating sustainable economic opportunities for Kenyan youth through solidarity-driven enterprises in the creative economy. We believe that every young person deserves the chance to build a meaningful livelihood while contributing to their community's growth.

Founded on the principles of collective action and mutual support, JENGE works to transform the landscape of youth employment in Kenya by providing access to capital, mentorship, and market opportunities through our innovative SESS model.

Why the Creative Economy?

Kenya's creative economy represents 5% of GDP and offers immense potential for youth employment. From fashion and design to music production and digital arts, these sectors are growing rapidly but lack the systematic support needed to maximize their impact.

With 67% youth unemployment and 703,000 jobs needed annually, traditional employment models are insufficient. The creative economy offers flexibility, entrepreneurship opportunities, and the chance for youth to build businesses aligned with their passions and talents.

How It Works

The Endowment Model: Rather than providing one-time grants, we're building a KES 1.2 billion endowment that will generate sustainable returns to support youth enterprises in perpetuity. This ensures long-term impact beyond the initial 15-year campaign.

Builder Commitments: We're mobilizing 55,000 "builders" - individuals and organizations who commit to annual contributions for 15 years. Each builder, regardless of tier, becomes part of a movement transforming youth employment in Kenya.

Enterprise Support: Endowment returns fund solidarity enterprises - businesses owned and operated by youth that prioritize both profit and social impact, creating jobs while building community wealth.

Our Impact to Date

Since launching the WE BUILD campaign, we've already begun transforming lives:

  • 42 builders committed to the 15-year journey
  • KES 1.52 million pledged towards Milestone 1
  • 150+ youth engaged in preliminary enterprise training
  • 3 pilot solidarity enterprises launched in Nairobi
  • Partnerships with 8 creative sector organizations
